What’s the best breakfast dish in the world? Is it the UK’s full English? America’s waffles and pancakes? France’s croissants and jams? China’s dim sum and dumplings? Or Japan’s rice, fish and miso soup? .
No, it’s an Indian dish by way of Portugal – Pav Bhaji. .
Menus often describe Pav Bhaji as “tangy mashed potato with Indian spices served with soft rolls”. With this in mind, you wont be shocked to hear that the dish is a potato based curry served with toasted soft bread rolls. .
The addition of the onions and lemon juice makes the dish. Cutting through the spice, they produce a mouth-watering combination of tangy umami and spices. So good is the Bhaji that there’s no need for the Pav, other than to bulk the dish out. .
Pav, Portuguese in origin (a literal translation being bread), are soft white rolls, while Bhaji is a vegetable curry from the Indian state of Maharashtra. The breakfast was born when Pav were introduced as a pairing for the Bhaji by the Portuguese when they controlled Maharashtra. .
Now my go-to breakfast when I'm in India, I’ve had multiple versions. In Jodhpur, I even came across a lamb mince version, but not even that could live up to the memory of the first one I had at Udaipur’s Lake Palace hotel @tajlakepalace . If you see this dish on a menu I can’t encourage you enough to try it, and be liberal with the shallots and lemon/lime or you’ll miss out. .

A world-famous fast food dish or perhaps the king of the street food from the western state of Maharashtra - Pav Bhaji is an amalgamation of vegetables gingered up with a unique blend of spices served with soft bread roll. Having originated during the thriving textile industry business in Mumbai, 
Pav Bhaji was invented as a midnight dish by street food vendors, made with all the leftover vegetables from the day, mashed up together, with loads of added spice, and butter. Bhaji is the Marathi word for a vegetable dish, and pav probably comes from the Portuguese word for bread. A mixture of vegetables mashed into a rich red-orange coloured spice mixture. Pav Bhaji is a flavourful combination of spicy and tangy, and is eaten with raw onions, a squeeze of lime and a dollop of butter.... .

Homemade Pav Bhaji is the best for a Sunday!  Added with extra butter and  some lemon...It totally makes my day !
Ingredients:-
1) Potatoes - 2
2) Cauliflower - half
3) Carrot - half 
4) Chopped Onion and Capsicum - according to ur taste
5) Chopped Tomatoes - 3
6) Grated garlic - 10
7) Green Peas 
8) Pav Bhaji Masala , Salt to taste , Kashmiri red chilli powder - All 2-3 tsp.
. :Steps and Procedure :-
1. Boil the veggies in a pressure cooker for 2 whistles on medium flame and keep the water left after the boiling in separate container. 
2. Mash all the veggies. Now heat a pan and add some butter n oil to it. Then add green chilli , ginger,garlic and fry it for a minute on medium flame.

3. Then add onions to the mixture and fry till soft and turning yellow. Stir nicely.

4. Add Chopped Capsicum and green peas . Mix it and fry for again a min after which u can add the tomatoes and stir it carefully.

5. Add some salt to taste and fry for 3-4 mins . After this , add kashmiri red chilli powder and pavbhaji masala to it .

6. Cover the pan and let it simmer for 30 - 40 sec so that all masalas get roasted well . At this point , add mashed veggies and mix well. 
KEEP MASHING THE BHAJI FOR THAT RICH FLAVOUR 
7. Now add some water which u kept separately and stir for sometime. Again add very less salt. 
8. Now  stir the mixture and cover the pan to let it simmer for 2-3 mins. It's Ready!........Add some butter and coriander leaves for the extra delicious flavour.
Don't Forget to add lemon too!! 9. Toast the pavs with butter and SERVE HOT with some chopped onions!! Thank me later 😂
